富岡製糸場
(Tomioka Silk Mill)

Homepage Info

The tomioka silk mill was built in 1872 for three main reasons. First was to import western technology on silk making. Second was for people from the west to be in charge of the place. Third to have people work there and to teach others after they know enough on how the system works. This silk mill used silkworms and needed a place close to the silkworm farms and close to water as that was a must when harvesting the silk. Having almost 55,392 square meters it was a large mill. By 2014 all of the building we’re either a national treasure or an important cultural asset. This place is also a world heritage site.
