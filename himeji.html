姫路城
(Himeji Castle)

Homepage Info

The Himeji Castle started construction in 1333 and was finished in 1609. The castle was never destroyed in war or any natural disasters but would be renovated due to the aging of materials. More recently it has been renovated to look like the picture above, glowing in the color of white. Before the sengoku era ended most castles were dark to hide themselves from wandering armies that wanted to invade the castle. After the sengoku era ends the castles after were painted white to make the illusion that the castle was bigger, showing the peace that the sengoku era has made. Naturally this building is a world heritage site and a national treasure. You can go inside the castle and go to the top floor which it was said the daimyou or owner of the castle lived.
